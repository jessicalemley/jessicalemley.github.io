My journey into UX/UI design

2006-2012

Psychological Operations Specialist, US Army Reserves

I've always been fascinated with how psychology influences our behaviors. Joining the Army as a Psychological Operations Specialist gave me an opportunity to explore that concept in a practical way while serving my country. While deployed to Iraq, I learned how design, messaging, and reshaping experiences can encourage positive behavioral change and empower people to improve their communities.

2014

Bachelor of Business Administration, Entrepreneurship - Kent State University

In college, I became enchanted with the idea of building products and services that people would love. As an entrepreneurship major, I learned what it takes to turn an untested idea into a viable business. Through various successes and failures, I came to understand how critical it is to incorporate constant customer feedback into all facets of the product development process.

2010-2015

Marketing & Graphic Design

I cut my teeth in marketing and design while working with tech startups in a wide range of fields. My work often required wearing multiple hats, which gave me first-hand exposure to the common challenges companies undertake when bringing products into the world. I gained a renewed appreciation for the exponential benefits of listening to users and stakeholders.

2015-2018

Marketing, Sales, Process Improvement

Working for a Lean-Agile tech consultancy, I got the chance to dive into the kinds of practices that can make or break companies and products. 2018-Present

User Experience/ UI Designer
